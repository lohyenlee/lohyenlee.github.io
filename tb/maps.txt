  asldfkjadslfj 
  <p class="indepth">
  This circuit implements a T flip flop, also known as a frequency divider.
  This essentially consists of two D latches and a NOT gate in series.
  On falling trigger, latch 1 closes and latch 2 opens, so X:=NOT Y.
  On rising trigger, latch 1 opens and latch 2 closes, so Y:=X.
  Thus the state (X,Y) cycles through the sequence 00, 10, 11, 01.
  The output (Y) toggles on the rising edge of the trigger.
  </p>

  <p class="indepth">
  This is a time-based pseudorandom number generator.
  When Mario steps on the pressure plate, a 2-tick pulse is generated.
  The period-8 clock generates a waveform that is ON for 4 ticks and OFF for 4 ticks.
  Together with the AND gates, this forms a pair of latches such that
    either the top latch is transparent and the bottom is opaque,
    or the top is opaque and the bottom is transparent.
  Either the top or bottom pulse extender gets activated.
  A 6-tick pulse is delivered to either the S or R input of the SR latch.
  </p>

<levelmap> <timestep>0</timestep> <name> S-4   T FLIP FLOP (13x5) </name>
<help>
USE THE P SWITCH TO TOGGLE THE LAST STAR.
<p class="indepth">
This circuit implements a T flip flop, also known as a frequency divider.
This essentially consists of two D latches and a NOT gate in series.
On falling trigger, latch 1 closes and latch 2 opens, so X:=NOT Y.
On rising trigger, latch 1 opens and latch 2 closes, so Y:=X.
Thus the state (X,Y) cycles through the sequence 00, 10, 11, 01.
The output (Y) toggles on the rising edge of the trigger.
</p>
