<p>TOP SWITCH LIGHTS STAR. </p>

<p class="indepth"> 
The two toads in the center form an
<a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flip-flop_(electronics)">SR latch</a>,
which is a bistable memory element.
The state can be controlled by sending a pulse to 
the Set (S) input or the Reset (R) input.
When S=1 and R=0, then O=1.
When S=0 and R=1, then O=0.
This latch is R-dominated, such that R trumps S.

<levelmap> <timestep> 20 </timestep> <name> 4-2   TIME IT RIGHT </name>
<help>
HIT THE P SWITCH WHEN THE BOTTOM STAR LIGHTS UP!
<p class="indepth">
Toggling the P switch causes the two inputs to the EQV gate to change from (0,1) to (1,0).
However, one input switches two ticks after the other, so the EQV gate outputs HIGH for two ticks.
Thus we have a pulse generator that triggers 
on both the rising edge and falling edge of the P switch signal.
</p>
