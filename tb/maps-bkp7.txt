<levelmap> <timestep>0</timestep> <name> 3-9    TOGGLY WOGGLY FLIPPY FLOPPY </name>
<help>
  TOGGLE YOUR WAY TO THE EXIT!
  <p class="indepth">
  This circuit implements a 
  <a href="https://www.build-electronic-circuits.com/t-flip-flop/">T flip flop </a>, 
  also known as a frequency divider.
  This T flip flop consists of two D latches in series with a NOT gate.
  Let X and Y be the outputs of the D latches.
  When T goes high, latch 1 opens and latch 2 closes, so Y:=X.
  When T goes low, latch 1 closes and latch 2 opens, so X:=NOT Y.
  The state (X,Y) cycles through the sequence 00, 10, 11, 01.
  The output (Y) toggles on the rising edge of the trigger.
  </p>

  <p class="indepth">
  The top half of the level is a pseudorandom number generator, implemented in Toad Logic.
  Stepping on the pressure plate activates a 2-tick pulse generator.
  There is a period-8 clock (a 2x2 square) generating a rapidly cycling square wave pattern.
  The clock signal, and its complement, control latches (AND gates).
  The 2-tick pulse passes through either the upper or lower latch, but not both.
  A pulse extender in each channel lengthens the pulse to 6 ticks.
  The 6-tick pulse drives an SR latch.
  The state of the SR latch (0 or 1) acts as an address to either of two 3-bit registers
  hardwired with contents 101 and 010 respectively.
  The pressure plate also triggers a copy operation from the selected register
