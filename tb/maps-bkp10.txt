  <h4> SR LATCH </h4>

    The two toads in the center form an
    <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flip-flop_(electronics)">SR latch</a>,
    which is a bistable memory element.
    The state can be controlled by sending a pulse to 
    the Set (S) input or the Reset (R) input.
    When S=1 and R=0, then O=1.

    This latch is R-dominated, such that R trumps S.

  <h4> SHIFT REGISTER </h4>
    <p class="indepth">
    A pair of D latches forms a pulse-triggered 
    <a href="https://www.build-electronic-circuits.com/d-flip-flop/">D flip flop</a>.
    A chain of D flip flops forms a
    <a href="https://www.build-electronic-circuits.com/shift-register/"> shift register</a>.
    This shift register consists of five D latches in series.
    The bottom switch controls the first star (x1).
    Switching off the right switch opens latches 1, 3, and 5, performing operations x2:=x1, x4:=x3, x6:=x5.
    Switching on the right switch opens latches 2 and 4, performing operations x3:=x2 and x5:=x4.
    Although there are 6 stars, this shift register only holds 3 bits of data 
      because the D latches are clocked alternately, causing every other bit to be overwritten.
    </p>

  <h4> T FLIP FLOP </h4>
    <p class="indepth">
    This circuit implements a 
    <a href="https://www.build-electronic-circuits.com/t-flip-flop/">T flip flop</a>, 
    also known as a frequency divider.
    This T flip flop consists of two D latches in series with a NOT gate.
    Let X and Y be the outputs of the D latches.
    When T goes high, latch 1 opens and latch 2 closes, so Y:=X.
    When T goes low, latch 1 closes and latch 2 opens, so X:=NOT Y.
    The state (X,Y) cycles through the sequence 00, 10, 11, 01.
    The output (Y) toggles on the rising edge of the trigger.
    </p>
</help>
